# naturally

## Origin and history of naturally
### naturally(adv.)

late 13c., "inherently, intrinsically, characteristically," from [natural](https://www.etymonline.com/word/natural#etymonline_v_2317) (adj.) + [-ly](https://www.etymonline.com/word/-ly#etymonline_v_32027) (2). From late 14c. as "in accord with natural law;" also "normally; usually, expectedly; as a matter of course, consequently, understandably." The notion is "as a natural result." From early 15c. as "without artificial assistance, by a natural process."
